Study Buddy



Overview

Project — 10 Weeks, Team Project
Team — Vicky Ly, Paridhi Khaitan
Scope — Product thinking, Interaction Design, Visual Design, Brand design
Tool — Figma


I designed a platform that help students find and share class resources easily and effectively.

Introducing... Study Buddy! A peer-to-peer sharing platform built to help UCSD students find class resources, including textbook, syllabus, lecture notes and more.

Background

During a 10-week interaction design class at UC San Diego, I worked with a team of three on the creation of a web app. Together, we went through the process of needfinding, user research, design and development. Within 10 weeks, we were able to design and develop a fully functional web application: UCSD Study Buddy.


UCSD Study Buddy, a peer-to-peer sharing platform, is built to help UCSD students find class resources, including textbook, syllabus, lecture notes and more.


At the end of the course, we decided to launch our application for UCSD students to access, and the site currently has great traction and has been receiving positive feedback.

Problem

71% of students have trouble finding the course resources in endless facebook threads.

Textbooks, lecture notes, practice exams and other resources are scattered in endless threads posted on Facebook groups (e.g. UCSD Textbook PDF Sharing). Students have trouble finding the information they need.

Design Q2 - Share class resources

How might we helping students share class resources?

HMW helping students share class resources?

Option A. Simple One-page Form

This design displays everything the user needs to fill out in one-page. By filling up the six fields in the page, students easily can share their textbook, lecture notes, syllabus, and practice exam.

Option B. Simple One-page Form

This design turns the list into friendly conversations. In each screen, the user only has to focus on one response. By filling up the six fields in each page, students easily can share their textbook, lecture notes, syllabus, and practice exam.

Option A vs. Option B

Our A design (one page upload) might be better because users can see every question they need to complete before submitting something. This allows them to gauge how much time they will need to spend to upload a resource. By knowing the time it takes to upload something, users can gauge the tradeoffs of time and want to upload something for others to use.
Our B design (fun, but multiple page upload) might be better because it emphasizes on the user's experience with the uploading process. While still being a quick process, users get a fun interaction when they are entering in answers and going to the next question versus a typical form formatted upload screen.

User Testing

Testing Procedure

We are testing the upload process of our site. This is where students will essentially fill out a form to upload whatever textbook they have.
This is a big component of our site and we want to make the experience users enjoy doing. Therefore, we are testing out different ways of filling out a form that could be interesting and captivating for a user.
We were testing following 3 different things:
01. The entirety of the site
02. Upload process #1
03. Upload process #2

User Feedback

Gus: “I prefer design B because it is simple, and I can understand what information I have to input in the first place.”

Andrew: “I like the interactive design of the longer process. I enjoy looking at the graphics while answering the field.”

Sam: “What’s the point of the longer format? It takes me longer to complete it..”

User insights

5/7 users prefer upload page option A - Simple One-page Form design. Users enjoyed the simplicity of the design and ability to glance all the questions at once.

Users leaned towards using the search bar first, rather than a scroll and find.

A/B Testing Results — Option A ✅

After understanding the user’s point of view on the upload process, we decided to move on to option A - simple one page form.
