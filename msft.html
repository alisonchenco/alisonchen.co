Empowering individuals to improve financial well-being



Overview


This summer, I joined Microsoft's Money in Excel team (virtually) as a Product Design Intern.

During the past 12 weeks with the cross functional team, we design and developed this feature. As a solo designer, I went through the end-to-end design process from researching, ideating, designing interfaces, conducting multiple rounds of internal + external user testings to iterating. It is great to see our feature being built and come alive. With this new experience, users are empowered to understand their spending in a novel and mindful way.


What I Worked on


Primary Project

Since my project is under NDA, I am not able to share the details. Here are some high level overview of what I accomplished:


I coordinated and led user research studies to validate our assumptions and ensure products align with user needs.

  • Identify research topics by working closely with Product and Engineering
  • Design qualitative and quantitative studies that address user behavior
  • Analyze user interview/usability and provide research insight updates.


I designed and evaluated new experience paradigms for product features.

  • Mapped out multiple user flows to show the end-to-end user journey.
  • Turn conceptual ideas into simple experience, and prototyped various interactive click-through prototypes.


I worked cross-functionally with Product and Engineering and constantly provided design perspectives and feedback.

Initiatives @Microsoft


Takeways


1. It is important to wear multiple hats (the researcher hat, PM hat, visual designer hat, etc.) as a designer.

2. Manage ambiguity through independent research and connecting with a cross-functional team.

3. Design is a team sport.
